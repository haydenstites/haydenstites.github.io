I've been using Blender for 4 years as my software of choice for anything 3D.  
This is a collection of some of my best renders, old and new.

<br>
<br>

## The Nautthorn Blade
{{< paige/image class="object-fit-cover rounded-4" src="/art/pirate.png" maxwidth="80%">}}
<br>
<p style="max-width: 65%; margin: 0 auto;"><i>My most recent completed render, and my first attempt at a human model.
The idea behind this piece is for a cursed pirate to be approaching the shoreline from the depths of the ocean.
The left side of the composition is gloomier than the right side, complimenting the theme of the mysterious pirate.
</i></p>

## Marsh
{{< paige/image class="object-fit-cover rounded-4" src="/art/marsh.png" maxwidth="80%">}}
<br>
<p style="max-width: 65%; margin: 0 auto;"><i>My oldest render that still holds up to my current standards.
My computer animation teacher critiqued one of my renders as not being realistic enough.
In response, I spent hours a day on this piece for over two weeks in secret.
Needless to say, this turned a lot of heads when I unveiled my final render.
</i></p>

## Camp
{{< paige/image class="object-fit-cover rounded-4" src="/art/camp.png" maxwidth="80%">}}
<br>
<p style="max-width: 65%; margin: 0 auto;"><i>After failing to create a quality scene in a low light setting, I felt the need to try again.
I wanted this piece to convey the vastness of the night, which is why most of the image focuses on the form of the wilderness.
The fire and occasional lantern act as a warm safe haven from the cold light of the moon, giving a sense of comfort and tranquility.
</i></p>
